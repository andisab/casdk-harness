## Example 1: Technology Report (Plain Text)

---

**Quantum Computing: Current State and Outlook**

Quantum computing has made significant strides in hardware, algorithms, and commercial deployment over the past 18 months, though practical quantum advantage remains limited to specific use cases.

**Hardware Developments**
Major advances in qubit count and error rates are emerging across multiple architectures. IBM's 433-qubit Osprey processor demonstrates improved coherence times, while Google's progress on surface codes for error correction shows promise for fault tolerance (ibm.com/quantum, google.com/quantum). Ion trap systems have achieved 99.9% gate fidelity in laboratory conditions (Nature, nature.com/articles/...). However, scaling to thousands of logical qubits needed for practical applications faces significant engineering challenges.

**Algorithms and Applications**
Quantum algorithms showing near-term promise include optimization for logistics, drug discovery simulations, and cryptography (McKinsey analysis). Several pharmaceutical companies are using quantum simulations for molecular modeling, with Roche and Biogen reporting productivity gains (biopharmadive.com). Financial institutions are exploring quantum machine learning for portfolio optimization, though most applications remain experimental (JPMorgan quantum-research).

**Industry Players and Investment**
The quantum computing market is projected to reach $8.5B by 2027, with major investments from tech giants and governments (MarketsandMarkets report). IBM, Google, Microsoft, Amazon, and Intel lead corporate efforts, while startups like IonQ, Rigetti, and PsiQuantum focus on specific architectures. China has invested over $15B in quantum research infrastructure, while the US CHIPS Act allocates $280M for quantum computing (NIST funding-announcements).

**Challenges and Timeline**
Key obstacles include error rates, qubit coherence times, and scaling manufacturing. Experts predict fault-tolerant systems capable of solving commercially relevant problems are 5-10 years away for most applications. Error correction overhead remains the critical bottleneck, requiring hundreds of physical qubits per logical qubit (Nature review-article).

**Summary**
Quantum computing is advancing rapidly in research but remains pre-commercial for most applications. Hardware improvements and algorithm development continue steadily, with specialized use cases in chemistry and optimization showing promise within 3-5 years. Widespread practical impact likely requires another decade of development.

## Example 2: Market Research Report (Markdown for Joplin)

# Electric Vehicle Market Analysis

## Executive Summary

The global EV market has reached an inflection point, with sales hitting 14% of total auto market in 2024 and battery costs achieving price parity with internal combustion engines. Momentum is driven by policy support, improving economics, and consumer preference shifts.

## Market Size and Growth

Global EV sales reached **18 million units in 2024**, more than doubling from 8.3M in 2022 ([BloombergNEF](https://bloomberg.com/energy)). Market penetration varies dramatically by region:

- **China**: 35% of new car sales (60% of global EV volume)
- **Europe**: 25% of new car sales
- **United States**: 9% of new car sales
- **Developing markets**: <2% penetration

The International Energy Agency projects EVs will represent **30% of global sales by 2030** under current policy trajectories ([IEA EV Outlook](https://iea.org/evoutlook)).

## Technology and Economics

**Battery costs** have fallen to **$115/kWh** in 2024, crossing the critical threshold for price parity with gasoline vehicles ([BloombergNEF battery-report](https://bloomberg.com/batteries)). Key developments:

\+ **Cost parity achieved** for most vehicle segments
\+ **Range improvements** with 300+ mile ranges becoming standard
\+ **Charging speeds** improving with 350kW ultra-fast charging networks
\+ **Battery chemistry** diversifying (LFP, NMC, solid-state in development)

\- **Charging infrastructure gaps** in rural areas and developing markets
\- **Grid capacity constraints** in some regions
\- **Raw material supply chain** concerns for lithium, cobalt, nickel

## Major Manufacturers

**Market Leaders:**
- **BYD** (China): 3.0M units sold in 2024, largest EV manufacturer globally
- **Tesla**: 1.8M units, leading premium segment
- **Volkswagen Group**: 1.2M units across multiple brands
- **GM, Ford, Stellantis**: Ramping production with $120B+ combined investments

**Emerging Players:**
- Rivian, Lucid (US startups)
- NIO, XPeng, Li Auto (Chinese new entrants)
- Hyundai/Kia showing strong growth

## Consumer Trends

McKinsey research shows **60% of consumers in major markets** now consider EVs for their next vehicle purchase, up from 40% in 2022 ([McKinsey automotive-survey](https://mckinsey.com/automotive)). Primary drivers:

1. Total cost of ownership advantages (fuel + maintenance)
2. Environmental concerns
3. Government incentives ($7,500 US federal tax credit, EU regulations)
4. Improving model availability and variety

## Outlook

The EV market has crossed the adoption tipping point in major economies. Expect continued rapid growth driven by:
- Expanding model lineups covering all segments
- Maturing charging infrastructure
- Ongoing cost reductions
- Tightening emissions regulations

Challenges remain in grid capacity, charging infrastructure, and supply chain resilience, but the transition trajectory is now well-established.

## Key Formatting Differences

### Plain Text Reports (.txt)
- Simple paragraph structure
- Inline citations with URLs
- No special formatting
- 500-800 words
- Professional but accessible tone

### Markdown Reports (.md) for Joplin
- Headers and subheaders
- **Bold** for emphasis on key metrics
- Bullet points with `+/-` for advantages/disadvantages
- Inline links `[text](url)`
- Tables where appropriate
- Slightly longer (can expand to 1000 words if topic warrants)

## Notes

- These are flexible examples showing different approaches
- Actual reports adapt to research findings and user context
- All claims require citations from research notes
- Report writer synthesizes across multiple research notes
- Length targets 500-800 words but can adjust based on content
